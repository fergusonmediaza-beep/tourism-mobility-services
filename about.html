Company Overview

A Platform Built for Africa's Tourism Future

Tourism Mobility Services (TMS) is a South African-based mobile super app designed to digitize and democratize tourism mobility. We consolidate ride-hailing, deliveries, professional concierge, fintech tools, and integrated safety into a single, user-friendly platform.

TMS bridges the gap between tourists seeking seamless, safe, and eco-conscious travel experiences and local operators in need of financial inclusion and income opportunities. Our platform is built on the belief that technology should empower communities, not extract from them.

Operating on a decentralized, operator-centric B2B2C model, TMS gives local entrepreneurs the digital infrastructure they need to compete, earn, and grow — from urban centres to villages, townships, and small dorpies.

Our Origin Story

TMS grew from Petro Shuttle, a shuttle service founded by Petro Maseko that demonstrated the urgent need for consolidated, safe, and technologically-enabled tourism mobility solutions during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Our Operating Model

The Decentralized B2B2C Approach

TMS operates as an aggregator e-commerce platform, connecting businesses and operators directly with consumers through transparent, equitable digital infrastructure.

B

Business

TMS as the technology infrastructure provider — the platform, tools, and ecosystem that enables everything.

2B

To Business

Local operators and tourism businesses who use TMS to reach customers, manage bookings, and earn income.

2C

To Consumer

Tourists and local travellers accessing a full suite of mobility, safety, and payment solutions through one seamless app.

Why Decentralized and Operator-Centric?

Traditional mobility platforms take 25 to 30% commission from every transaction, effectively taxing the livelihoods of the operators they depend on. TMS rejects this model entirely. By operating a zero-commission structure and providing operators with premium digital tools, marketing support, and coupon-based income streams, we create a true partnership economy — not an extractive one.
